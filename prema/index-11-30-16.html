UI/UX & Developement Process

Simple Planning

What do you want to build and who's using it? What do you want to accomplish? That's my plan of attack. On the left side of the infograph is where my marketing experience and web developement kick in, followed by a punch of mind blowing graphics. This knockout combination is the ringer for marketing and business objectives before the development process even begins.

Personas, Profiling, & User Scenarios

Q: But why do we need to create personas and profile users?
A: We need to think about who your target audience is and what their social demographics are. Basically, We need to put ourselves in our users shoes, then we'll need to create user scenarios that explain what their problem is and how we can solve it.

Research, Observe, & Test

Before we layout the groundwork, let's put our user through some simulations. Let's take a close look at what we think our users will be doing in our environment: Will they take the path we want them to follow? What will their experience be like?

Wireframing, Sketching, & Prototyping

Now that we have a good understanding of our user and conceptualized their experience, let's start skething and mock things up! For fast wireframing and prototypes I use Sketch and Balsamiq. I can also use other programs or methods to demostrate user flows.

Refined mockups

Once the low-fidelity mockups are approved, We'll breath some life into these rough comps and start creating the digital graphics using Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe XD, Sketch, or several other programs. During this process we'll create clickable prototypes using Invision and provide documentation assets for programming.

Finalize, Deploy and Test

After receiving prototypes and specs, It's time to get in the lab and start coding using the latest and proficient programming languages. When programming is complete, your application will be deployed to a server and will continue to run through vigorous testing. It will return to the Research, Observe & Testing stage to refine the user experience.
